Meatloaf

Description

This is the meatloaf I started making when I was just a teenager loosely based off one from the restaurant that I worked in. It's a perennial family favorite! It's wonderful sliced cold the next day and used in a warm, toasted sandwich with a slice of provolone and a little butter.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Meatloaf mix (1/3 Beef, 1/3 Veal, 1/3 Pork)
  • 1 teaspoon Pepper
  • 1 packet Onion Soup Mix
  • 3 slices leftover bread (heels are great), torn into bite-size pieces
  • 1 tablespoon Parsley
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire Sauce
  • 2 cups beef stock

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  2. Combine beef, pepper, onion soup mix, bread pieces, parsley, Worcestershire Sauce in a bowl and knead together until items are mixed evenly
  3. Form into an football-shape and place into a roasting pan
  4. Pour stock over the meat mixture
  5. Roast for 40 minutes or until the internal temperature is 165 degrees farenheit
  6. Let rest for 10 minutes and serve
  7. Enjoy!
